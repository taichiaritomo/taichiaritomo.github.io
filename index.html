How do size, form, and color interact and compete as parts of a whole? I developed a simple, browser-based design tool in an attempt to describe these perceptual relationships explicitly.

The tool continuously processes the text-editor (left) as an image and generates its saliency map (right). The intensity of white corresponds to the likelihood that a human viewer will gaze at that point first, according to a computer vision algorithm that models visual attention. By providing real-time feedback on changes to the design of information, the tool aimed to foster a sort of collaboration between a human designer and a mechanical "viewer".
Payment and Subscription Interfaces for SoundCloud Aug 2016
More info
When SoundCloud was expanding its subscription product offering, I worked with the product team to map the new billing logic and user-flows. I re-designed web and mobile interfaces for payment and subscription management with two main goals: (1) Responding to feedback from user-testing and community forums by making billing and cancellation more transparent. (2) Creating a flexible interface that could accommodate a wide range of payment methods, user types, and subscription terms.

I designed some of the necessary evils of SoundCloud's mobile app—new ads in the stream. This is the Framer prototype I built to demonstrate how users would interact with video ads, and how they would handle audio playback handoff. For a music app, it was important that the listening experience would transfer seamlessly with ads. You can demo it to see how music is faded and resumed as the video is activated and closed.
TEDxRutgers conference poster Aug 2017
More info
TEDxRutgers is a student-run organization that hosts an annual TEDx conference and other smaller events open to the community. This is one of the posters I designed and illustrated for the 2017 Conference. Because TED is a conference with broad perspectives, we didn't want its visual representation to be confined to specific examples of tech, entertainment, or design. Following the theme of footnotes, we chose instead to depict a process: engaging with something to appreciate it more closely, like we often did as kids. A "sister" poster, illustrated by fellow designer Jessica Zhu, depicted a scene at the beach, zooming in to fine particles of sand and shells.

Research on urban development Apr 2018
More info
Retail chains can provide underserved communities access to jobs, low prices, and a broad range of goods and services that are only made possible with the scale of a transnational corporation. However, these corporations often have little concern for the neighbors of their brick-and-mortar outposts beyond their role as a consumer base. Retail chain development can also increase surrounding property values and threaten local independent businesses, producing the threat of displacement. This illustration served as the title image of a presentation on the possible outcomes of retail development.
