Encoded Ink Dots

for artistsspace.org, ~April 2019

Laurel proposed that on each artist's page, a unique dot would appear in the tiny favicon space in your web browser. I wrote an algorithm to create the unique shape from each artist’s name: Each letter in the name is extended along a starburst. Then, the corners are rounded to resemble an ink blot.

Summer 2018, sketching at GSD Design Discovery

It feels liberating to give up control. Like sitting in the back of a bus, with a pencil that's about to fall out of your hand. Or, like trying to forget what a flower looks like, to draw something that reminds you of a flower.

May 2019, at home

If you ever forgot what an analog clock did, you could recover its meaning by watching the hands sweep by at different speeds, cataloguing the shapes they make together during the day and the shapes they’ll never make. Clocks are meaningful because each moment of the day corresponds uniquely to a configuration of hands, a lossless encoding.

I made a flower clock. Every second, the current time is encoded as a unique flower, so that each flower appears once, and then you’ll never see it again. The shapes are similar to the ink blots, except the input (time) also controls how the curves stretch and turn, producing more unpredictable shapes.
