Hi,

My name is Taichi Aritomo. I'm a Japanese-American in Fort Lee, New Jersey.


About me

Since late-2018, I've been programming websites with Beautiful Company, the graphic design practice of Laurel Schwulst, and volunteering at the Adaptive Design Association. For a while, I've also been teaching informally as a tutor, workshopper, or summer school teacher. These exposures got me interested in programming and improvisation, how the decisions we record into technology can complement our aspirations, our growth, and the surprises of reality.


About this website

Last year, I realized the importance of being seen... Gardening in the spring, I got closer to my neighbors just by being outside every day. Working on my grad school applications, I grew a lot by trying to tell my friends about my intentions and work. Sharing little moments of my day on Instagram, I enjoyed getting responses from people I hadn't spoken to in a long time. All of these things made me curious about ways to participate in networked culture so I can grow sustainably with others.

This year, I'm thinking more about this website's visibility and trying to show connections to other people and events that surround and support the things I'm doing. It's always a work in progress, but currently in the works: improvements in accessibility (February) and a 'friends' section for admiring out loud (March).

Thanks for reading (:
