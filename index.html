How do size, form, and color interact and compete as parts of a whole? I developed a simple, browser-based design tool in an attempt to describe these perceptual relationships explicitly.

The tool continuously processes the text-editor (left) as an image and generates its saliency map (right). The intensity of white corresponds to the likelihood that a human viewer will gaze at that point first, according to a computer vision algorithm that models visual attention. By providing real-time feedback on changes to the design of information, the tool aimed to foster a sort of collaboration between a human designer and a mechanical "viewer".
Payment and Subscription Interfaces for SoundCloud Aug 2016
More info
When SoundCloud was expanding its subscription products, I worked with the product team to map the new multi-tiered billing logic and possible user-flows. For the expansion I re-designed interfaces for payment and subscription management. Responding to feedback from user-testing and community forums, the designs were aimed to make billing and cancellation more transparent.

TEDxRutgers conference poster Aug 2017
More info
TEDxRutgers is a student-run organization that hosts an annual TEDx conference and other smaller events open to the community. This is one of the posters made for the 2017 Conference. Because TED is a conference with broad perspectives, we didn't want its representation to be confined to specific examples of tech, entertainment, or design. Following the theme of footnotes, we chose to illustrate a process: that of engaging with something to appreciate it more closely, like we often did as kids. A "sister" poster, illustrated by fellow designer Jessica Zhu, depicted a scene on a beach, zooming in to fine particles of sand and shells.
